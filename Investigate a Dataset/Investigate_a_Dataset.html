Introduction¶

In this project , This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment.

This data sets contains recordes whether patients miss appointments , attributes of patients and information about appointments (original source on Kaggle)

Question¶

  • Are no-show appointments associated with a certain gender?
  • Who in terms of age misses more appointments?
  • Are chronic illnesses like hypertension and diabetes affecting patient's absences?
  • Is alcoholism a prominent factor in patient's absences?
  • Do SMS reminders decrease the number of absences?

Conclusions¶

Results¶

1)Attend to scheduled appointment isn't depending on patient age

2)Receive a message about the appointment may help a little bit

3)People that have a scholarship attends in there appointment more than other

4)Female attends in there appointment more than male , female have scholarship more than male which make sense about people that have a scholarship attends in there appointment more than other and female attends in there appointment more than male 5)Female suffer from diabetes more than male

6)Male suffer from Alcoholism more than female

7)Probability of attending male that suffer from Alcoholism is the least

8)People that don't suffer from Alcoholism or Diabetes have the most probability to miss their appointment

limitations¶

1)Most of our variables are categorical , which does not allow for high level of statistical method

2)We don't have alot of details for certain factors to draw conclusiins

3)Can't show strong correlation between factors since most of our data is categorical
